Nuclear Bomb
A nuclear bomb is a powerful explosive device that releases energy through a nuclear reaction, either by splitting atoms in a process called fission or by combining them in a process called fusion. These reactions produce immense destructive power through blast, intense heat, and lethal radiation. The immediate effects are devastating, and the long-term consequences include persistent radioactive fallout, which can cause widespread damage and environmental disruption.
